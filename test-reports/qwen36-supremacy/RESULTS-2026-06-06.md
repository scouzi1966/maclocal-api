# Qwen3.6-27B-4bit — MLX engine benchmark (2026-06-06)

Re-run of the cross-engine suite on the **latest** of every engine, MLX-only (llama.cpp dropped —
not MLX), plus a feature-fair **MTP speculative-decode** head-to-head.

## Setup

- **Hardware:** M4 Pro, 64 GB unified memory.
- **Model:** `mlx-community/Qwen3.6-27B-4bit` (same weights every engine). MTP run uses
  `Youssofal/Qwen3.6-27B-MTPLX-Optimized-Speed` (byte-identical base + `mtp.safetensors` head).
- **Method:** `probe.py` (streaming, 200-tok decode + ~1800-tok prefill; tokenizer-agnostic) is the
  **canonical decode** number. `llama-benchy` (pp512/tg128 at depths 0/4096/8192/16384, 2 runs) for
  depth scaling. One engine on the GPU at a time.

## 1. Decode throughput (canonical probe, tok/s)

| Engine | decode tok/s | vs afm |
|--------|------:|------:|
| LM Studio | 15.74 | +0.4% |
| **afm** | **15.68** | — |
| rapid-mlx | 15.38 | −1.9% |
| oMLX | 15.35 | −2.1% |
| mlx_vlm 0.6.2 | 15.21 | −3.0% |
| ollama | 13.87 | −11.5% |

Plain autoregressive decode is **a near-dead-heat across the MLX servers** (top 5 within ~3.5%);
afm is in a statistical tie for first with LM Studio. ollama (NVFP4 quant) trails ~12%.

> oMLX's llama-benchy `tg` reads ~33 tok/s — that is a **known measurement artifact** (the harness
> documents it); its real decode is the probe value 15.35.

## 2. Decode vs context depth (llama-benchy tg128, tok/s)

| Engine | d0 | 4k | 8k | 16k | d0→16k |
|--------|----:|----:|----:|----:|------:|
| **afm** | 15.38 | 15.24 | 14.59 | **14.30** | −7.0% |
| mlx_vlm | 15.10 | 14.70 | 14.32 | 13.97 | −7.5% |
| ollama | 14.44 | 14.57 | 14.37 | 14.08 | −2.5% (flattest, lowest) |

afm has the highest decode at every depth among the depth-swept engines and holds 14.3 tok/s at 16k
(the SDPA 0.31.3 backport keeping long-context decode up). LM Studio / rapid-mlx are probe-only this
run; oMLX benchy is unreliable (see note).

## 3. Prefill & prefix cache (tok/s)

| Engine | prefill cold | prefix-cache HIT |
|--------|------:|------:|
| oMLX | 125.1 | — |
| LM Studio | 124.7 | — |
| **afm** | 119.7 | **3149 (97% hit)** |
| mlx_vlm | 116.5 | — |
| ollama | 107.6 | — |
| rapid-mlx | (probe anomaly) | strong (vllm_mlx prefix cache) |

Cold prefill is again a cluster (~107–125). afm's distinguishing feature is the **server-level prefix
cache**: a repeated prompt prefix re-prefills at ~3149 tok/s (~26× cold) — the multi-turn / agentic
path none of the other servers exercised here.

## 4. MTP speculative decode — feature-fair head-to-head

Only afm and mlx_vlm implement Qwen3.6's **in-model MTP head**. Greedy, non-streaming, 200 tokens,
same prompt, same base weights.

| Engine | AR tok/s | MTP tok/s | speedup |
|--------|------:|------:|------:|
| **afm `--mtp`** | 15.8 | **23.3** | **+47%** |
| mlx_vlm `--draft-kind mtp` | 15.5 | 23.0 | +49% |

afm's MTP (23.3 tok/s) **matches the official mlx_vlm MTP** (23.0) — within ~1%, afm marginally
ahead — and both deliver ~+48% over AR. mlx_vlm reports 72.4% draft acceptance, 2.44 tokens/round.

## Bottom line

- **Plain decode:** afm is tied for the fastest MLX server on Qwen3.6-27B (15.68 tok/s, #1–2 of 6).
- **Long context:** afm leads decode at every depth and degrades least to 16k among swept engines.
- **Prefill:** competitive cold; uniquely strong with its prefix cache (~26× on repeats).
- **MTP:** afm's speculative decode is on par with the reference mlx_vlm implementation (+47%),
  i.e. afm gives ~**23 tok/s** on Qwen3.6-27B — the fastest configuration measured here, tied with
  mlx_vlm-MTP and ~48% ahead of every engine's autoregressive decode.
